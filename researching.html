Researching

My publications have focused on technology and learning, information ethics teaching, and using software to model ethics as it occurs, as opposed to how it should occur.

My unpublished research has focused on understanding how individuals in groups solve problems, how individuals think through problems, and emulating these processes using software. The areas of knowledge that I have linked or am linking include individual characteristic social psychologies; small group, discourse, and speech act theories; what we know about problem solving, decision making, and reasoning; and artificial intelligence and cognitive architectures.
