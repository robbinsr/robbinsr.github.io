Overview

This portfolio shares work I have done in three primary areas: Teaching, Researching, and Learning.

I have taught (which means "learned from") about 3000 undergraduate and graduate students in traditional academic settings. The skills my students have practiced are software engineering, analytics, information systems management, project management, and software application use.

I have researched technology use for learning, complex problem solving, and simulating complex problem solving using software. One form of complex problem solving is learning, from the learner's point of view. Simulating problem solving using software has forced me to bring together artificial intelligence, cognitive science, computational linguistics, and the semantic web.

I am learning or have learned about many techniques and technologies, particularly recently. Recent and current learning has focused on using algorithms to learn from data. Recent and current technologies I have learned are R, Python, and their respective ecosystems. Knowledge I gained earlier fell into the categories of performing research, engineering software, and managing projects.
